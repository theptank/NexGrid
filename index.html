The Problem

Energy infrastructure is under pressure it wasn't designed for

Utilities manage thousands of critical assets across vast geographies. As climate volatility accelerates, reactive maintenance is no longer sufficient.

01

Climate volatility is accelerating

Extreme weather events — floods, heat waves, storms — are increasing in frequency and severity, placing unpredictable stress on grid infrastructure built for a more stable climate baseline.

02

Networks are growing more complex

Distributed generation, expanding transmission demands, and aging assets create a monitoring burden that existing inspection regimes can't address at the required scale or cadence.

03

Operators remain largely reactive

Most utilities still rely on scheduled maintenance cycles and post-failure inspection. Risk is assessed after damage occurs, not before. That model cannot scale to the demands of the energy transition.

04

Outage and maintenance costs are rising

Unplanned outages carry direct costs in restoration, regulatory exposure, and reputational risk — while deferred maintenance compounds underlying vulnerability across the asset base.

The Solution

Intelligence built from Earth observation data

NexGrid fuses multiple environmental and spatial data streams into a unified risk intelligence layer for energy network operators. Every asset. Continuously monitored.

Data inputs

Satellite Imagery

Multi-spectral and SAR imagery at sub-weekly cadence. Vegetation change, terrain deformation, inundation, and thermal anomaly detection at asset level.

Environmental Signals

Real-time and forecast weather data, soil moisture, flood modelling, extreme event probability, and seasonal risk scoring derived from global climate datasets.

Terrain & Geospatial Analysis

LiDAR-derived elevation, drainage basin modelling, proximity risk scoring, and geospatial context for every asset in the operator's infrastructure network.

Infrastructure Risk Modelling

Predictive failure likelihood scores per asset, maintenance prioritisation ranked by risk exposure, and portfolio-level resilience mapping.
